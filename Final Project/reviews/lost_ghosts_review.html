Lost Ghosts by Red Scalp

Album Review by Joseph Faria

Red Scalp is what you would get if Black Sabbath decided to have a native american theme throughout their music. This new album features five new tracks all with ominous themes of the supernatural and nature. This album is a wild ride from start to finish. Heavy guitar riffs, native american chanting, as well as saxophone solos are prominent on the album.

The album starts off with Portal, a slow-building heavy song that builds tension til it leads into the next song. The next song Faces. The heavy sounding guitar carries this song with decent vocals throwned in. My favorite song on the album was Mantra Bufala. The song features Native-American chants alongside a great guitar riff. Easily the best song on the album.

This album only featured 5 songs, but Red Scalp managed to deliver in the meantime. There was no second wasted on this album. I only recently discovered Red Scalp by stumbling on to them through You-tube. This album was certainly a good introduction to the band and I look forward to hear more of the music in the future.

Final Rating: 3/5
